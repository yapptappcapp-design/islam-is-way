Islam Is Way

Hajj: A Step-by-Step Guide

Hajj is the fifth pillar of Islam, obligatory once in a lifetime for those who are physically and financially able. This guide covers the essential rituals according to the Qur'an and Sunnah.

وَأَذِّن فِى ٱلنَّاسِ بِٱلْحَجِّ يَأْتُوكَ رِجَالًۭا وَعَلَىٰ كُلِّ ضَامِرٍۢ يَأْتِينَ مِن كُلِّ فَجٍّ عَمِيقٍۢ

"And proclaim to the people the Hajj; they will come to you on foot and on every lean camel; they will come from every distant pass." (22:27)

1
Ihram الإحرام

Before crossing the Miqat, enter the state of Ihram:

  • Purification: Perform ghusl (ritual bath), clip nails, remove unwanted hair.
  • Clothing: Men wear two white unstitched sheets (izar & rida). Women wear modest, loose clothing (no niqab or gloves).
  • Intention & Talbiyah: Make the intention for Hajj (e.g., Hajj Tamattu, Qiran, or Ifrad) and recite the Talbiyah frequently:
    لَبَّيْكَ ٱللَّهُمَّ لَبَّيْكَ، لَبَّيْكَ لَا شَرِيكَ لَكَ لَبَّيْكَ، إِنَّ ٱلْحَمْدَ وَٱلنِّعْمَةَ لَكَ وَٱلْمُلْكَ، لَا شَرِيكَ لَكَ
    "Here I am, O Allah, here I am. Here I am, You have no partner, here I am. Verily all praise, grace and dominion are Yours. You have no partner."
Prohibitions during Ihram: No cutting hair/nails, using perfume, hunting, sexual relations, or arguments. Be extra mindful of your behaviour.
2
Tawaf al-Qudum طواف القدوم

Upon arriving in Makkah, perform the Tawaf of arrival (for non-residents):

  • Enter the Masjid al-Haram with your right foot, reciting the du'a for entering a mosque.
  • Begin Tawaf from the Black Stone (Hajr al-Aswad), making takbir and touching/kissing it if possible, or simply pointing.
  • Circle the Kaaba 7 times anti-clockwise. Men uncover the right shoulder (Idtiba) and walk briskly (Raml) for the first 3 circuits.
  • Between the Yemeni Corner and the Black Stone, recite: "Rabbana atina fid-dunya hasanatan wa fil-akhirati hasanatan wa qina adhab an-nar."
3
Sa'i between Safa & Marwah السعي

After Tawaf, proceed to perform Sa'i (walking between the hills of Safa and Marwah):

  • Start at Safa, face the Kaaba, make du'a.
  • Walk towards Marwah (one lap). Men run between the green markers.
  • Continue until you complete 7 laps (Safa to Marwah is one, Marwah to Safa is another).
  • Make du'a throughout; there are no specific prescribed supplications.
For Hajj Tamattu: After Sa'i, men should shave or trim their hair, and exit Ihram. You will re-enter Ihram on the 8th Dhul Hijjah.
4
Day of Tarwiyah (8th Dhul Hijjah) يوم التروية

Proceed to Mina before Dhuhr prayer:

  • Enter Ihram if you had exited (for Tamattu pilgrims).
  • Spend the night in Mina, praying Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, Isha and Fajr, shortening Dhuhr and Asr (2 rak'ahs each) but not combining.
  • This is a Sunnah day; use it for worship and preparation.
5
Day of Arafat (9th Dhul Hijjah) يوم عرفة

The most important day of Hajj. After sunrise, head to the plain of Arafat:

  • Combine and shorten Dhuhr and Asr prayers at Dhuhr time.
  • Stand in sincere du'a, repentance, and remembrance of Allah until sunset. The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Hajj is Arafat."
  • Face the Qiblah, raise your hands, and pour your heart out. No specific du'a is required.
  • Do not leave before sunset.
6
Muzdalifah (Night of 10th) المزدلفة

After sunset, move calmly to Muzdalifah:

  • Combine Maghrib and Isha prayers (Maghrib 3 rak'ahs, Isha shortened to 2).
  • Spend the night under the open sky. Collect pebbles for Rami (7 for the first day, 21 for each of the next days).
  • Perform Fajr prayer and make du'a until just before sunrise.
7
Rami al-Jamarat & Sacrifice (10th Dhul Hijjah) رمي الجمرات

Proceed to Mina after sunrise for the busiest day:

  • Rami: Throw 7 pebbles at Jamarat al-Aqaba (the largest pillar), saying "Allahu Akbar" with each throw.
  • Sacrifice (Hady): Offer an animal (sheep, goat, or share in a cow/camel). Eat from it and distribute to the poor.
  • Halq/Taqsir: Men shave their heads (preferred) or trim; women cut a fingertip's length of hair.
  • After this, you exit the state of Ihram partially (first release), allowing normal clothing but marital relations remain prohibited until Tawaf al-Ifadah.
8
Tawaf al-Ifadah طواف الإفاضة

Return to Makkah to perform Tawaf al-Ifadah (the essential Tawaf of Hajj):

  • Perform Tawaf (7 circuits) as before, but without Idtiba and Raml for men (unless performing Tawaf al-Qudum for the first time).
  • After this Tawaf, all restrictions of Ihram are lifted, including marital relations.
  • If you haven't performed Sa'i after Tawaf al-Qudum, you must do Sa'i now.
Important: Tawaf al-Ifadah is a pillar of Hajj and cannot be omitted. It can be done on the 10th, 11th, or 12th, but earlier is recommended.
9
Days of Tashreeq (11th-13th) أيام التشريق

Spend the nights of the 11th and 12th (and optionally 13th) in Mina and perform Rami each day:

  • After Dhuhr, stone all three Jamarat (small, medium, large) with 7 pebbles each.
  • Start with Jamarat al-Ula (smallest), make du'a; then al-Wusta, make du'a; finally al-Aqaba (do not stop after this one).
  • If you stay for the 13th, repeat the stoning after Dhuhr.
  • You may leave Mina on the 12th before sunset if you wish, but staying the 13th is Sunnah.
10
Tawaf al-Wada (Farewell) طواف الوداع

Before leaving Makkah, perform the Farewell Tawaf:

  • Circle the Kaaba 7 times as a final act of worship.
  • No Sa'i or Raml required. Make du'a for acceptance and safe return.
  • This is the last thing you do before departing; women in menses are exempt.

اللهم تقبل منا إنك أنت السميع العليم

O Allah, accept from us. You are the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing.
